# Crosstalk Lab — Spoken Elevator Speech

---

Most AI tools are built around one idea: you ask, it answers. One model, one voice, one perspective. That's useful, but it has a ceiling.

Crosstalk Lab is built around a different idea. Instead of asking one model, you run the same question through Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini simultaneously — and then you use the *differences* between their answers as the actual data. Where they agree, you probably have solid ground. Where they diverge, you have something worth investigating.

The framing I use is triangulation. Surveyors don't measure a point directly — they take bearings from multiple positions and let the geometry find it. Crosstalk does that epistemically. You're not looking for the best answer from any single model. You're looking for what the space of answers tells you.

There's also a structured dialogue layer. You can route one model's response to another — so Claude reacts to what Gemini said, or ChatGPT pressure-tests Claude's argument. The session becomes a conversation between models, moderated by you.

It's not a productivity tool. It's a sense-making tool. The goal isn't to get tasks done faster — it's to think better.
