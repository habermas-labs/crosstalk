## Points of Divergence

### Technical Precision Versus Evocative Resonance
A fundamental tension emerged between Claude's initial preference for technically descriptive names and the other models' embrace of purely metaphorical language. Claude's progression from "Hexascope" to "The Prism" to "The Bridge" shows a real-time calibration toward CT's naming philosophy, while ChatGPT and Gemini more readily adopted evocative language from the beginning. This divergence illuminates the challenge of balancing precision with poetry in specialized vocabularies.

### Active Versus Passive Metaphors
The models split on whether generative roles should be characterized by active agency or natural process. ChatGPT's "Mirrorweaver" and Claude's "The Bridge" emphasized active construction and translation, while Gemini's "The Medium" suggested facilitation and channeling. Similarly, Claude's preference for "Flux and Field" over "Warp and Weft" reflected a bias toward dynamic forces rather than structural elements. This tension reveals different assumptions about the nature of AI agency in collaborative meaning-making.

### The Role of Cultural Specificity
While all models agreed that "Yin and Yang" was too culturally specific for CT's framework, they disagreed about the acceptable degree of cultural reference. Gemini's "The Crystal" drew explicitly on mineralogy and sacred geometry, while ChatGPT's alternatives ("Spark and Current," "Seed and Catalyst") remained more abstractly relational. This divergence suggests ongoing uncertainty about how specialized vocabularies should balance cultural accessibility with conceptual precision.
