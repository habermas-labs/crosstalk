## Notes

### S-01 — Dimensionality ladder
First articulated at the end of the "feature suggestions v2" thread (late session, pre-sleep). The cleanest pitch scaffold CT has: intuitive, visual without a diagram, creates natural stopping points for different audiences. Each step is a genuine dimensional expansion, not just "more." 2D+ is notable because it's dynamic triangulation — the conductor's directed participation changes what the models can see, it's not just a traffic cop. 3D is the Bakhtinian flip: the human stops being external observer and becomes the fourth vertex whose outsideness is structurally load-bearing. Belongs in `ct-tetras.md` as the framing context, and possibly also in a standalone note since the ladder applies to the full taxonomy, not just Tetras. Cross-reference: ethics-of-becoming.md (EoB as 2nd-order framework analogy).

### S-02 — `ct-tetras.md`
The permanent note that doesn't exist yet. Should define: what a Tetra is (four-role configuration, three AI + human conductor), the four-vertex geometry, the dimensionality ladder as context, the single-word naming convention (concept word captures the epistemic act), and then host each named Tetra with its role assignments and intent. Currently this knowledge is distributed across tracker entries (072, 073, 076), session logs, and the taxonomy note. Consolidating it is the priority output of the dedicated Tetras thread.

### S-04 — Reciprocity Tetra
Human as permanent Summarizer keeps the session at 2D+. True 3D requires the human to rotate into one of the other roles (Predictor, Questioner, Clarifier), which means a model must temporarily hold the Summarizer/meta position. This is a significant design implication — it's the clearest example of the observer/participant toggle (S-12) in a specific Tetra context. Role rotation suggestions by the models themselves (S-14) are most natural here because Reciprocal Teaching's original design explicitly built in rotation.

### S-05 — The Agora Tetra
Two-layer design: a curated set of philosopher/thought-leader presets (Socratic method, specific thinkers) plus an open user-defined slot. The abstraction level question is unresolved: "Argue like Kant" vs. "hold the Kantian deontological position" — different epistemic moves with different pedagogical implications. Label does work: "Aristotle" invites curiosity or superficiality; "the virtue ethics position" is more honest about what the model is actually doing. Academic genealogy note from tracker: Socrates → Plato → Aristotle makes the Agora the right starting point (not the Lyceum or the Academy). Human conductor as questioner = human in Socrates' own role.

### S-06 — Calibration
May function better as a modifier applied to other Tetras than as a standalone configuration. Could run a Reciprocal Teaching session *in calibration mode* — each role also carries a register assignment. Key unresolved question: who assigns registers? Teacher as conductor, or is there a self-assessment component? The self-assessment angle connects to metacognition research and is worth pursuing in the dedicated thread. Universal design principle (S-18) is central here: accessibility features designed for specific needs demonstrate consistent broad utility. This is the argument for Calibration having value beyond accommodation.

### S-07 — The In-Between Tetra
Intent: scaffolded suspension of epistemic tension. Roles actively resist the pull toward synthesis or resolution. Discomfort of genuine not-knowing treated as the productive state. Philosophically connects to Winnicott's transitional space, Vygotsky's ZPD as productive incompletion, Bakhtin's heteroglossia. Distinct from RIP (which expands surface area through divergence) — the In-Between holds tension *between* already-articulated positions rather than generating new ones. Structural role design is genuinely open. Deserves its own permanent ZK note: `the-in-between.md` (flagged in tracker 076).

### S-08 — Discourse Tetra
Reframe from "debate" to "debate prep / practice lab" resolves the tribunal problem. AIs are sparring partners before the match, not judges during it. Critical pressure is *in service of* the human advocate, not directed *against* them. Steelman role (friction-adjustable, S-16) is the key design piece. Possible two-version or toggle design: observer mode (human at meta, AIs in plane) vs. participant mode (human holds advocate role, AI holds meta). The inversion relationship with Porosity is structural and worth documenting: Discourse = human projects outward into opposition field; Porosity = human creates conditions for outside to come in.

### S-09 — Porosity Tetra
Osmosis is the right metaphor: membrane that selects based on concentration gradients, not force. Not breaking echo chambers — changing their permeability. Role sketch: assumption-excavator (surfaces what's underneath the user's view, doesn't argue against it), alternative-coherence (offers a different system that might address the same underlying concerns — not antithesis, but alternative), change-witness (tracks what's actually shifting in the user's expressed thinking across the session). Human conductor is fourth vertex but also the subject of inquiry in a way unique to this Tetra. Steelman inversion (S-15): in Porosity the Steelman translates the user's position into a new register rather than challenging it, and closes with the summary. Guardrails question (what constraints do models have on political discussion) identified as a test to run before finalizing role design.

### S-10 / S-11 — Cartographer role and RIP Tetra
Cartographer: maps idea terrain mid-session. Flag-and-hold behavior: surfaces thematic recurrence and tangential threads without demanding the session stop. Prompts the group to notice and decide how much to follow a path — agency stays with the conductor. The interrupt behavior is AI-native: ambient attention across a fast-moving generative session is cognitively expensive for humans and cheap for models. Role name should be used in UI with metaphor explained in documentation. RIP Tetra needs the Cartographer as a structural requirement — the idea coordinate system (A1, B1, etc. from earlier taxonomy work) is the Cartographer's output format.

### S-15 — Steelman inversion (Porosity)
In Discourse: Steelman finds strongest version of the *opposing* argument → productive friction for advocate preparation.
In Porosity: Steelman finds strongest version of the *user's own view* expressed in a new register → translation, not pressure. Question it asks: "How would someone who doesn't share your cultural context but shares your underlying values describe what you're trying to say?" Positions the Steelman as the closing role in Porosity because they've been tracking how the user's position has been heard and reflected throughout — best positioned to give the most accurate account of where things landed.

### S-20 / S-21 — Altitude metaphors and CT as epistemic instrument
10,000-foot view names both gain (larger patterns) and loss (granular detail, direct participation) — the honest version of the observer position. Forest/trees inversion: too close = can't see the forest; too far = can't see the trees. 2D+ toggle is a mechanism for choosing altitude deliberately. Human sensory limits as frame: perception is always resolution-limited by distance; microscopes and telescopes extend range without replacing natural perception. CT's observer/participant toggle as epistemic instrument of the same kind — extends the range of available positions. Connects to vnenakhodimost' (outsideness as epistemic distance as a resource) and to the dimensionality ladder. Candidate permanent ZK note, probably connected to both the ladder note and `vnenakhodimost-surplus-of-seeing.md`.
